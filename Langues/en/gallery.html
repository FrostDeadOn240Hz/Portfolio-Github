The Visual of Inazuma

Since its modest beginnings on Nintendo DS with pixel art sprites, the Inazuma Eleven saga has always pushed the visual limits of portable consoles. Today, with Victory Road, the franchise has taken a monumental step forward by adopting a high-definition graphics engine that blurs the line between video games and Japanese anime. Each super technique is an explosion of colors and particle effects, paying homage to the “cel-shading” style that made Level-5 famous. The stadiums are no longer simple flat fields, but lively arenas with dynamic crowds and realistic lighting effects that change depending on the time of the match. The character design, still supervised by Takuzo Nagano, retains its distinctive round and expressive style, but gains depth and detail in the clothing. This gallery takes you on a journey through these developments, from preparatory sketches to the dazzling final renderings that will thrill long-time fans and newcomers alike.
